Crinum lilies (Crinum spp.) are large, heat and moisture loving plants, producing an abundant array of showy flowers in summer. Foliage is spirally arranged, coarse and open. It is often used for a short, growing hedge. Crinum Lily Special Feature Borders, containers, houseplants, pond or water garden peripheries. Good accent. Good cut flower. Crinum Lily Uses Ornamental Use: The plant is used for an ornamental purpose Medicinal Use: Bulbs are extremely acrid When roasted, they are used as a rubefacient in rheumatism Crushed and toasted bulb is applied to piles and abscesses to cause suppuration The juice of the leaf is used in earache
